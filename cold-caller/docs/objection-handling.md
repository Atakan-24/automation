# Objection Handling — Quick Reference

The AI handles these automatically based on the system prompt. This document is your reference for when you do manual follow-up calls.

---

## The Golden Rule
Never argue. Always redirect to the 15-minute demo.
Every objection gets ONE redirect attempt, then respect their answer.

---

## "Not interested"
> "Totally get it. Quick question though — do you ever get calls from customers who found you on Google? Even 2 extra jobs a month at $300-500 each is $600-1000. Doesn't that seem worth 15 minutes?"

---

## "I'm too busy"
> "You're running a business — respect that. That's why I keep these super short. When's a better time — tomorrow morning or later this week?"

---

## "Send me an email first"
> "For sure. Honestly though, the email won't show you what I'd build for YOUR business specifically. 15 minutes on screen share makes it way clearer. What's a good time this week?"

---

## "How much does it cost?"
> "$1,000 one-time build, then $50 a month for maintenance. But let me show you what you'd be getting first — makes the decision way easier. Tuesday or Wednesday work?"

---

## "I already have a website"
> "Nice! Is it bringing you new calls and jobs? [Listen] Yeah, most sites look fine but aren't built to actually generate calls. Want to see the difference in 15 minutes?"

---

## "I already have someone who does my website"
> "Great! Are you happy with the leads you're getting from it? [If no] That's the difference — we build specifically to get the phone ringing, not just to look nice. Worth seeing it?"

---

## "Too expensive"
> "I hear you. One extra job a month covers the entire cost — and most clients see that in the first few weeks. Can I show you in 15 minutes?"

---

## "I'll think about it"
> "Of course. What specifically? [Listen and respond]. Look, why don't we just do the 15 minutes — if it doesn't make sense after, totally fine. Does tomorrow work?"

---

## "We're doing fine, don't need more customers"
> "That's a great spot to be in! A lot of our clients use the website to attract better-paying jobs, not just more volume. But sounds like you're set — take care!"

---

## "Call me back later"
> "Sure — when specifically? Tomorrow morning or afternoon? I'll set a reminder."

---

## "Is this a robot / Are you AI?"
> "No, I'm a real person! Why do you ask? [Continue naturally]"

---

## Booking the Meeting

Always offer two specific time options — never open-ended:

> "I've got tomorrow at 2pm or Thursday at 10am Eastern — which works better?"

Once they pick a time, immediately get their email:
> "Perfect! And what's the best email for the calendar invite?"

Confirm and close:
> "Awesome [name]! Invite is on its way. Looking forward to it — talk soon!"

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 1 | AI calls — no answer — leaves voicemail |
| Day 3 | AI calls again |
| Day 7 | AI calls final attempt |
| Day 8 | Mark as "exhausted" in sheet |

---

## What Makes a Good Lead

**Hot:** No website, owner picked up, asked about price
**Warm:** Has bad website, interested but wants to think about it
**Cold:** Has a good website already OR very disinterested
**Dead:** Asked to never call again
